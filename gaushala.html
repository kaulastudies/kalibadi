Animal Welfare & Environment

Maa Annapurna Gaushala

A sanctuary for abandoned cattle, preserving indigenous breeds and promoting zero-waste organic farming in Kashi.

About the Project

Today, countless cows are left to wander the streets, surviving on garbage, suffering from diseases, and falling victim to road accidents. This reality is a tragic departure from our ancient ethos where the cow was revered as a symbol of life and sustenance.

The Maa Annapurna Gaushala is designed not merely as an animal shelter, but as a holistic, self-sustaining ecosystem. Spanning a significant portion of our 30-acre campus vision, this Gaushala will rescue abandoned cattle, provide immediate veterinary care via an ambulance service, and preserve indigenous Indian cattle breeds.

Sustainability & Impact

  • ✓ Rescue & Rehabilitation: Safe haven for dairy-discarded and accident-prone cattle with a dedicated veterinary dispensary.
  • ✓ Zero-Waste Economy: Production of Bio-CNG, biogas, and organic fertilizers (Jivamrit) to support local agriculture.
  • ✓ Breed Preservation: Maintaining the genetic purity of hardy, disease-resistant indigenous breeds.
  • ✓ Women's Employment: Creating jobs for rural women in the production of dung pots, bricks, and handicrafts.
